Everyone loves a good game of music bingo, right? That must be why it is one of the fastest growing entertainment choices of bars and restaurants It is super easy to get started, and everyone loves a good playlist!

For those who have never played music bingo before, let me give you a quick rundown on how the game is played. Players are given bingo cards, but unlike traditional bingo cards that contain nothing but numbers, the squares on music bingo cards are filled with song titles or the names of music artists. The music bingo host plays a couple minutes of a song, and the players have to match the song played with one of the squares on their cards.

Does it sound like fun? It is, trust us. Plus, it can be super challenging! Many of the cards we use in our games aren’t all that straightforward, and the clues can be a bit tricky. Plus, we just love to play themed games like our newest set: Zoo Rock.

The Zoo Rock includes all your favorite hits with animals in the title, and there are more songs featuring animals than you probably knew existed! The Zoo Rock playlist includes songs like the Foo Fighter’s Monkey Wrench or Ginuwine’s Pony. See where this is going now?

These music bingo cards are available for instant download in the trivia store, and you can follow our playlist on Spotify. Easy peasy. You can be hosting your Zoo Rock show tonight! What are you waiting for?

Trivia Night Isn't Just For Bars, You Know

4/21/2018

0 Comments

 
Many bars or pubs host a trivia night, but that isn't the only venue that can rock a show.

via GIPHY

Trivia night is a great way for bars and restaurants to bring in extra money, but running a pub isn't the only reason to host a trivia show. Think outside of the trivia box! 

Hosting a trivia night is an excellent way for bars or pubs to bring in extra money on a slower night of the week. A game generally lasts around 2 hours which is 2 extra hours of having people sit around ordering drinks in an establishment, it’s a pretty safe bet that the entertainment will lead to profits.

However, bars aren’t the only places that a trivia show can be a big win. There are many venues and events that could use the fun and entertainment that a trivia show can bring! While this list isn’t exhaustive, it is a great introduction as to ways a trivia night could be used in places other than the local pub.

School Fundraisers 

Raising money for school children is a noble cause to be sure, but it can be tricky to actually find a fundraiser that people are interested in supporting. The little catalogs full of overpriced junk is a hard sell, and the average bake sale doesn't bring in all that much cash. So, what is a school to do? Host a trivia night, of course! Local businesses will likely donate a few items that can be given away as prizes, and for a small cover charge, parents can have a fun game show trivia night! It is a win for everyone.

Family Reunions

Family reunions are often large events with a lot of people that don’t always have that much in common, well, besides being related, and after all the eating is finished it can often be hard to find acceptable entertainment for all the various age groups. That is why having a trivia night makes for the perfect plan! General knowledge trivia is perfect for the mixed age groups, and playing against family members always brings out the competitive streak in people. Just remember to keep it civil or next year’s reunion could be a bit awkward.
​

Meetup Groups

Meetup groups are an excellent way to get out and meet new people, make new friends with similar interests. A lot of them are very topic specific, and may even be groups that belong to a specific fandom. Hosting a themed trivia night at a local restaurant or meeting hall makes for a great meetup event! People will need to form teams and collaborate on the answers which is an excellent way to socialize without the pressure of coming up with conversation topics all on your own.

Dorm or First Day of School Mixers 

Starting a new year in a new class or in a new dorm can be a bit nerve-wracking. Teachers and RAs are often looking for games to break the ice or get people mingling, and an interactive trivia game is the perfect solution! Students will find themselves conversing with their peers in no time.


Corporate Team Building Events 

There is no need to go on some fancy retreat and practice your trust falls when you can bring the team building to your conference room! You can build your trivia teams from the different departments or mix everyone up and get them out of their comfort zone, either way, an afternoon playing game show trivia is more fun and more cost effective.

Holiday Parties 

Hosting a big holiday function but don’t know what to do for entertainment? Trivia games can be themed to the celebratory holiday to fill your entertaining void. Games can run smoothly with just two or three teams, so even if it is a fairly small party, the game can still go on!
​

Senior Centers

Many assisted living facilities and senior centers run successful bingo nights, but why not try a trivia night as well? The game can be tailored to the age group of the players for a fun night!

Charity Events

Just like the school fundraisers, charity events can harness the power of trivia to help them raise some cash. Trivia can be for a fancy black tie event too!

Youth Groups

It can be tough to find games that are age appropriate and still a lot of fun, but trivia is so east to customize, it is always a hit. Interactive games with light-up buzzers and a playlist full of popular artists will have all the kids begging for the next trivia night.

See? Hosting a trivia night is perfect for just about any occasion, and with the downloadable games in the Fat City trivia store, anyone can set up a show. Release your inner trivia host and organize your show today.

19 Music Bingo Games Our Crowds Can't Get Enough Of

4/11/2018

2 Comments

 
Music bingo unites the music lovers and trivia buffs in one fun event, and our bingo hosts know how to keep the crowd entertained with tricky cards and amazing playlists. Check out this list of our crowd favorites!

via GIPHY


It is hard to pick our favorite music bingo playlist because they are all so much fun, but these nineteen bingo games seem to be a sure bet with the crowds. Go ahead, ask one of our music bingo hosts or our loyal game players! They'll tell you these games make for the best music bingo shows anywhere!


For The Older Crowd

  • Life's A Beach: Those California girls down in Margaritaville drinking the tequila with a lime in a coconut, am I right? Although not everything in this category is from way back when, it has enough golden oldies to keep the retired crew digging the vibe with artists like The Beach Boys, Jimmy Buffet, and Don Henley on the list.
  • Golden Oldies: Speaking of golden oldies...yep, we've got a whole list dedicated to the classics. Bring on the classic entertainers like Elvis, Otis Redding, and good ol' Patsy Cline.
  • The 70's: A tumultuous decade that inspired artists to create some timeless tunes. This playlist includes songs from Joni Mitchell, Queen, and Lynyrd Skynyrd.
  • The Beatles: A band so iconic they get a category unto themselves. No one will be able to resist singing along when you play the Fab Four's classic hits like Yellow Submarine, Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds, and Blackbird.

Family Friendly Shows

  • Movie Soundtracks: These movie tunes span the decades which makes this the perfect game to play with a family crowd. Everyone knows which movie My Heart Will Go On comes from!
  • Disney: Got a lot of kiddos in the crowd? We've got a playlist for that too! Get the room excited when you hand out bingo cards full of Disney classics like Circle of Life, Under the Sea, and yes, even Let it Go!
  • TV Shows: Theme songs can be awesome (Fresh Prince of Bel Air), catchy (Scooby Doo), or down right iconic (Game of Thrones), but no matter the category, this game is perfect for the family team. It has a little something for each age group!
  • X-mas: Hey, everyone loves a good Christmas carol even in July. A holiday themed music bingo game with all your favorite tunes sang by all your favorite artists.

The Mixed Bag

  • Food Fight: At a new venue and you aren't sure about the vibe yet? Try a mixed, theme game! Different songs from different periods and genres, but they all have food in common! From a classic like Blueberry Hill to some gangsta rap by Snoop Dogg, everyone can play along.
  • Girls vs Boys: We got songs about girls like Girls Just Wanna Have Fun, and we got songs about boys like Boyz-n-the-Hood. Who will reign supreme?
  • Out of This World: Space the final frontier...of music bingo! We've got Space Cowboys, Star Wars, and Drops of Jupiter up in this theme. Far out, man.
  • One Hit Wonders: A classic music trivia category! Everyone knows these mega-hits, but do they know who sang them? Here's a freebie Tainted Love was a hit by a band called Soft Cell. You're welcome.

A Decade of Hits

  • And Then There Was Hair: Who doesn't love an old-fashioned rock ballad? The hair bands ruled the 80's, and we have an entire bingo card dedicated to those luscious locks. Poison, KISS, and Guns-n-Roses all have a square here.
  • Oh...the 90's: Was grunge the death of hair? The eternal debate! We aren't here to decide it though, we are just here to deliver these classic 90's hits from artists like Gin Blossoms, Beck, and No Doubt!
  • 2009 Billboard: Kanye's gonna let you finish, but first...here are the hits of the 09's! We've got Gaga, we've got Swift, and oh yes, we've got the man himself!

The Tough Stuff

  • Nicknames: Do you know the King of Pop? How about the Queen of Soul? Who is known as The Boss? This game isn't for the faint of heart! It can be a tough round, but the trivia fans will eat it up.
  • Countries: How many people know where their favorite artist hails from? This card names the country, you need to figure out the artist and where they come from. If we play Blissing Me, for instance, the player has to know the artist is Bjork AND know that she comes from Iceland. Not so easy is it?
  • Antonyms: Your clever players will love this one! The card may say something like "Stop You Down" which is the correct clue for Rolling Stones' Start Me Up! Can you figure them all out?
  • Anagrams: This is hands down our hardest game, and it is only recommended for your serious players as casual ones tend to get a bit frustrated. The answers are jumbled on the card into different words like Pearl Jam turns into Maple Jar. Whew. How'd we come up with this one?

There you have it folks! A collection of our music bingo crowd favorites in a tidy list just for you. Now to pick which game to play...
