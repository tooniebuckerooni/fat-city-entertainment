Everyone can play music bingo. There are no hard questions, no arguing over answers, and no one sitting out — if you can hear a song, you're in the game. That's why venues that try it keep it on the calendar.

But there's a difference between playing songs off a phone and running a show. The Music Bingo Handbook is the playbook we wish we'd had when we started: the setup that works the first time, the pacing that keeps drinks flowing, and the little host moves that turn a Tuesday crowd into regulars.

Questions, answered

The ones we hear most.

What format is the Handbook?

A downloadable PDF you can read on any device or print. You get it instantly after checkout, and it's yours forever — including updates to the same edition.

Do I need special equipment or an app?

No app. Music bingo runs on printed cards, a music source, and a way to play sound. The Handbook covers exactly what to use at every budget level, starting with gear you may already own.

Does it come with bingo cards and playlists?

The Handbook is the how-to guide. Ready-to-play card games (each with matching streaming playlists) are available in our Trivia Store — and the Handbook shows you how to get the most out of them, or make your own.

I run a venue but don't want to host. Can you just do it?

Depending on where you are, yes — get in touch and we'll either host it or help you train someone who can.
